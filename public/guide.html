📊 Sector Rotation Scanner — User Guide

A simple guide to understanding what this tool shows you and how to act on it.

⚡ Quick Start (30 seconds)

Here's all you need to do:

  1. Open the dashboard.
  2. Look at the green "BUY" rows at the top. These are ETFs (exchange-traded funds — basically baskets of stocks you can buy like a single stock) where conditions are favorable.
  3. Check the confidence percentage. Higher = more factors agree. Stick to 65% and above if you want high-conviction ideas.
  4. Read the rationale column. It tells you why in plain English.
  5. If you own something marked SELL or TRIM, consider reducing or exiting that position.

That's the basics. The rest of this page explains what's behind those signals.

💡 The Big Idea (No Jargon)

Think of the stock market like a building with different rooms — Technology, Healthcare, Energy, Gold, Bonds, etc. Money flows between these rooms constantly.

This tool watches which rooms money is walking into (good sign for those sectors) and which rooms money is leaving (bad sign). It also checks whether a room just started filling up (early, good time to enter) or is already packed (late, crowded, risky).

The ideal situation: money is flowing into a room that's not yet crowded. That's your opportunity.

🚦 Understanding the Signals

The 4 Actions

SignalPlain EnglishWhat You Should Do
BUY Conditions are favorable — money is flowing in and the trend is young Consider buying this ETF
HOLD No strong signal either way If you own it, keep it. If you don't, wait for a clearer signal.
TRIM It's gone up a lot already — the easy gains are done If you own it, sell some (maybe half) to lock in profits
SELL The trend is broken — this is going down and likely to continue If you own it, sell. If you don't, stay away.

The Confidence Number

Think of confidence like a weather forecast: "70% chance of rain" means several indicators agree it'll rain, but it's not guaranteed.

  • 65-80%: Multiple factors agree. These are your best opportunities.
  • 50-64%: Some evidence, but not overwhelming. Smaller position if any.
  • Below 50%: Weak signal. Don't act on these alone.

Every investment goes through a cycle, like seasons. Knowing which "season" you're in tells you whether it's a good time to get in:

⬆ Early Uptrend 🌱 Spring — things are just starting Best time to buy. The trend is fresh, lots of upside potential.
⬆ Established ☀️ Summer — full bloom Good to buy on dips. Trend is proven and healthy.

🌍 Asset Class Flows (The Big Picture)

Before picking a sector, check the big picture: is money going into stocks at all, or is it fleeing to safety?

Example: Risk-Off (market scared)

US Equities → OUTFLOW

Bonds → INFLOW

Gold → INFLOW

Translation: Investors are selling stocks and buying safe assets. Not a great time to buy aggressive sectors. Favor defensive sectors (Utilities, Healthcare) or bonds/gold.

Example: Risk-On (market confident)

US Equities → INFLOW

Bonds → OUTFLOW

Gold → OUTFLOW

Translation: Investors are moving money into stocks from safe havens. Good environment for growth sectors (Technology, Consumer Cyclical).

🔄 Sector Rotation (Where Within Stocks?)

Even when stocks overall are doing fine, some sectors outperform while others lag. This rotation tells you where the smart money is going within the stock market.

Key patterns to watch:

  • Growth sectors leading (Technology, Consumer Cyclical, Communication Services) → Market expects the economy to grow. Bullish sign.
  • Defensive sectors leading (Utilities, Healthcare, Consumer Defensive) → Market is getting nervous. Money hiding in safe stocks. Could precede a broader decline.
  • The "Rank Δ" column shows momentum. A positive number means that sector is improving faster than its peers. It's climbing the leaderboard.

📋 Step-by-Step Example

Scenario: You open the dashboard and see this at the top:

BUY XLU (Utilities) — Confidence: 75%

"New uptrend forming with capital inflow confirmation"

Here's how to think about it:

  1. What is XLU? It's the Utilities sector ETF — power companies, water companies. Boring but steady.
  2. Why BUY? The tool detected that Utilities just started going up (early uptrend) AND money is flowing into it (inflow confirmed). Two signals agreeing = 75% confidence.
  3. Check the big picture: Look at Asset Class Flows. If US Equities shows OUTFLOW and bonds show INFLOW, this is a defensive rotation — Utilities makes sense as a safety play.
  4. How to act: Buy shares of XLU in your brokerage. Decide how much based on confidence — maybe a full position at 75%, half position at 60%.
  5. When to sell: When the signal changes to TRIM or SELL, or when the trend phase shifts to "Extended" or "Early Decline."

🤔 Why Sector ETFs Instead of Just Buying the S&P 500?

You might wonder: "Why bother with sector rotation? Can't I just buy SPY (the S&P 500) and hold it forever?"

You absolutely can. Buying and holding SPY is a perfectly valid strategy — it's what most financial advisors recommend. Over long periods, the S&P 500 has returned about 10% per year on average.

Sector rotation is for people who want to do slightly better by tilting toward what's working and away from what's not. Here's the difference:

ApproachWhat You DoProsCons
Buy & hold SPY Buy the whole market, ignore the noise Simple, proven, no effort You ride every downturn fully. You own the losers along with the winners.
Sector rotation Overweight sectors with momentum, avoid sectors in decline Can outperform in sideways/rotating markets. Avoids worst-performing sectors. More effort. Can be wrong. Higher trading costs.

When sector rotation helps most: In markets that aren't going straight up. When Technology is falling but Healthcare is rising, SPY might be flat — but you'd make money if you were in Healthcare and not Technology.

When just buying SPY is better: In strong bull markets where everything goes up. Sector rotation can cause you to miss the rally by being in the "wrong" sector at the wrong time.

A reasonable middle ground: Keep most of your money (70-80%) in SPY or a total market fund. Use 20-30% for sector tilts based on this dashboard. That way you capture the market's gains regardless, and the sector bets are a bonus.

🎯 Should I Buy the ETF or Individual Stocks?

When the dashboard says "BUY XLI (Industrials)", you have two choices:

OptionExampleRiskPotential Reward
Buy the sector ETF Buy XLI (holds 70+ industrial stocks) Lower — one bad stock won't hurt much Moderate — you get the sector average
Pick individual stocks Buy Caterpillar, UPS, or Honeywell Higher — one stock can drop 20% on bad earnings Higher — a single winner can gain 50%+

When to use the ETF (recommended for most people):

  • You're new to investing
  • You don't have time to research individual companies
  • You want diversification — if one company has bad news, the others cushion the blow
  • You're making a sector bet, not a company bet

When individual stocks might make sense:

  • You already follow specific companies and understand their business
  • You want to avoid certain stocks within a sector (e.g., you like Tech but not a specific company)
  • You're comfortable with higher volatility for potentially higher returns
  • You have a smaller amount to invest and want concentrated bets

Important: This tool does NOT analyze individual stocks. It tells you which sectors have favorable conditions. If you want to pick individual stocks within a favored sector, you'll need additional research — check earnings, valuation, and company-specific news.

🚫 Common Mistakes

  • Don't buy Extended signals. If something is already way up, you're late. Wait for a pullback or look elsewhere.
  • Don't ignore SELL signals hoping it'll recover. A broken trend usually gets worse before it gets better. Cut losses early.
  • Don't chase bounces in downtrends. A "Counter Trend Bounce" is not a recovery — it's a trap that fools people into buying before the next leg down.
  • Don't put all your money in one signal. Spread across 2-3 BUY signals rather than betting everything on one sector.
  • Don't check obsessively. This updates once per day after market close. Checking more often doesn't help.

⚠️ What This Tool Cannot Do

  • It cannot predict surprises. Earnings announcements, Fed decisions, wars, and pandemics can override any signal overnight.
  • It looks backward. It measures what has already happened. "Early uptrend" is the best it can offer — that's still based on recent price moves, not crystal-ball predictions.
  • It doesn't know your situation. Your risk tolerance, time horizon, and existing portfolio matter. A 25-year-old and a retiree should act differently on the same signal.
  • Signals change. Today's BUY can be next week's HOLD. Check back regularly but don't panic over day-to-day shifts.

📖 Glossary (Terms You'll See on the Dashboard)

TermWhat It Means in Plain English
ETFA basket of stocks you can buy as one ticker. Like a pre-made portfolio.
RSIA 0-100 score. Above 70 = it's gone up too much too fast (due for a drop). Below 30 = it's been beaten down (might bounce).
SMA 50 / SMA 200The average price over the last 50 or 200 days. If price is above this line, the trend is up. Below = trend is down. Think of it as a "average mood" line.
Relative VolumeIs trading activity higher or lower than normal? High volume = lots of conviction behind the move. Low volume = weak, don't trust it.
Inflow / OutflowMoney moving INTO something (people buying) or OUT OF something (people selling).
Sector RotationThe natural cycle where different parts of the market take turns leading. Like a relay race — different sectors take the baton.
Mean ReversionThings that go too far from their average tend to snap back. Like a rubber band stretched too far — it's going to snap back eventually.
BreadthWhat % of stocks in a sector are going up. High breadth = healthy, broad rally. Low breadth = only a few big stocks carrying the weight (fragile).
Flow ScoreA single number summarizing capital movement. Positive = money coming in. Negative = money leaving. Bigger number = stronger signal.
