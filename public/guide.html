📊 Sector Rotation Scanner

A daily-updated dashboard that tracks where institutional money is flowing across sectors and asset classes, classifies trend maturity, and generates actionable buy/sell signals.

Investment Philosophy

Follow the Money, Respect the Trend

Markets move in rotations. Capital doesn't disappear — it flows from one sector or asset class to another. The goal is not to predict the future, but to identify where money is moving right now and whether that move is early enough to be actionable.

This scanner is built on three principles:

  1. Capital flow direction tells you what to watch. If money is flowing into Utilities and out of Technology, that's a defensive rotation — pay attention.
  2. Trend maturity tells you when to act. An inflow signal into a sector that's already 15% above its 50-day moving average is late — you've missed the easy money. An inflow into a sector just crossing above its 50-day MA is early — that's opportunity.
  3. Conviction comes from convergence. A single indicator is noise. When capital flow, trend phase, RSI, and moving average alignment all point the same direction, confidence increases.

This is a rotation tool, not a stock picker. It tells you which sectors and asset classes have favorable conditions for new positions. You still need to pick individual stocks or use the sector ETFs directly.

How to Read the Dashboard

SELL Trend broken or confirmed downtrend Exit positions, don't try to catch the bounce

Confidence ranges from 35% to 80%. Higher confidence means more factors are aligned. Focus on signals with 65%+ confidence — these are the high-conviction setups highlighted at the top of the page.

What Makes a High-Confidence BUY?

  • 75% — Early uptrend + capital inflow confirmed. Best risk/reward: new trend with money behind it.
  • 70% — Pullback in an existing uptrend with depressed RSI. Mean reversion entry in a proven trend.
  • 65% — Established uptrend with sustained inflow. Trend is your friend.
  • 60% — Early uptrend, but no flow confirmation yet. Promising but unconfirmed.
  • 45% — No clear trend but inflow starting. Speculative.

Practical Workflow

  1. Check the High-Conviction Signals first. These are the setups where multiple factors converge. If there are BUYs at 65%+, those deserve your attention.
  2. Cross-reference with Asset Class Flows. A sector BUY in a risk-off environment (equities outflow) is less reliable. A sector BUY in a risk-on environment has tailwinds.
  3. Check the Trend Phase. Prefer buying into Early Uptrend or Pullback in Uptrend. Be cautious of Extended — even with a BUY signal, you may want to wait for a pullback.
  4. Use sector ETFs for execution. The simplest approach: buy the sector ETF (XLK, XLF, XLU, etc.) directly. For higher conviction, pick individual stocks within the favored sector.
  5. Review SELL and TRIM signals for existing positions. If you hold something flagged SELL with 70%+ confidence, consider exiting rather than hoping.

What This Tool Does NOT Do

No tool replaces judgment. This scanner identifies conditions, not certainties. Key limitations:

  • No earnings or fundamental analysis. A sector can have great technicals and still drop on earnings surprises.
  • No macro event awareness. Fed meetings, tariffs, geopolitical events can override any technical signal.
  • Lagging by nature. Moving averages and performance data look backward. Early signals are the best this can offer — they're still reactive, not predictive.
  • Daily granularity. Data updates after market close. Intraday moves aren't captured.

Data Sources & Methodology

What's Tracked

Sectors — All 11 S&P 500 GICS sectors via SPDR Select Sector ETFs (XLK, XLF, XLE, XLV, XLY, XLP, XLI, XLB, XLC, XLRE, XLU).

Asset Classes — 7 classes via 13 ETFs: US Equities (SPY), Bonds (TLT, IEF, SHY), Cash (BIL), Gold (GLD, GDX), Commodities (DBC, USO), Crypto (IBIT, BITO), International (EFA, EEM).

Stock-level data — ~500 S&P 500 stocks with 1-week, 1-month, and 3-month performance, market cap, and sector classification.

How Signals Are Generated

The rotation score combines four normalized factors:

  • Rank shift (35%) — is the sector climbing or falling in relative performance rankings?
  • Momentum acceleration (25%) — is short-term momentum diverging from the longer trend?
  • Institutional spread (20%) — are large-cap stocks outperforming small-caps (sign of institutional buying)?
  • Breadth divergence (20%) — is the percentage of positive stocks expanding or contracting?

The flow score blends multi-timeframe performance (20% weekly, 50% monthly, 30% quarterly) with a capped volume confidence multiplier to avoid distortion from volatile/new ETFs.

The action signal synthesizes trend phase, RSI, capital flow, and moving average alignment into a single recommendation with confidence level.
